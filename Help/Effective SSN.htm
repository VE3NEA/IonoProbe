Effective SSN

The Effective SSN (SSNe) is based on ionospheric F-layer observations rather than solar observations. It represents the true state of the ionosphere and reflects the effects of both solar radiation and geomagnetic disturbances.

Effective Sunspot Number: the value of the sunspot number that provides the best match between the mathematical model of the ionosphere and the observed critical frequencies of the F-layer.

IonopPobe downloads real-time ionosonde data from the NOAA web site once an hour and calculates the SSNe by fitting the IRI-2001 ionospheric model (URSI coefficients) to the 6-hour block of observations.

DX Atlas v.2.2 and higher automatically reads the SSNe value from IonoProbe and generates a real-time F-layer critical frequency map.
