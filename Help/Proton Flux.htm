Proton Flux

High energy protons emitted by the Sun reach the Earth and, moving along the magnetic field lines to the polar regions, hit the polar cap areas. Polar cap absorption (PCA) events result from the ionisation of the D-layer of the polar ionosphere by high energy protons. PCA events cause HF radio blackouts for trans-polar circuits and can last for several days.

Proton Flux: The proton flux at energies greater than 10 MeV expressed in particles per cm2 per steradian per second.

Use the Proton Flux value as an indicator of the PCA events.
