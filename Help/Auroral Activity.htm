Auroral Activity

When a solar wind blow reaches the Earth, magnetospheric electrons accelerated by the electric field hit the atmosphere and create additional ionization in the areas around the magnetic poles (Auroral zones).

Auroral activity index: An estimation of the total power input by auroral particles to the polar regions on the scale of 1 to 10

The radio propagation paths that cross the Auroral zones may be degraded because of increased absorption of the radio signal.

The Auroral activity index can be used as a direct input into DX Atlas for a real-time map of Auroral zones.

DX Atlas v.2.2 and higher automatically reads the Auroral activity index from IonoProbe and displays a real-time auroral activity map.
