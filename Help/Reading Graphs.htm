Reading Graphs


Changing the View

Space weather data are presented in IonoProbe in the form of Graphs on three tabbed pages, Day, Week, and Year. Switch between the pages by clicking on the tabs.




The slider at the bottom of the window displays the horizontal time scale for the graphs and allows scrolling the graphs by clicking on the buttons with arrows or by dragging the slider. Also, you can scroll the graphs by dragging them horizontally.


Elements of the Graph



1 - The parameter name. Click on it to view a brief description of the parameter and its impact on the HF propagation. See the Understanding Parameters section for a more detailed discussion of all space weather parameters.

2 - Storm threshold

3 - Current time marker

4 - The current value of the parameter

5 - The vertical scale. Units are shown on a mouse-over hint

6 - Measured value

7 - Specific to the Kp graph. Since 3-hourly Kp values are not available until the end of the 3-hour period, IonoProbe downloads and displays 1-hourly Kp values that are later replaced with a single 3-hourly value.

8 - Estimated value


Reading Values

As you move your mouse cursor over the graph, the mouse-over hint displays the time and the value of the parameter under the cursor. <no data> is displayed for the missing values; (estimated) is added to the estimated values.
