X-Ray Flux

A burst of X-ray radiation from the Sun covers all sunlit area of the Earth and causes extra ionization of the D-layer which results in higher absorption of HF radio waves. This is called a Short-Wave Fadeout (SWF) or a Sudden Ionospheric Disturbance (SID).

X-Ray Flux: X-ray flux in the 1 to 8 Angstrom range expressed in Watts/m2.

The X-ray Flux parameter displayed in IonoProbe can be used as an indicator of the SWF events.
