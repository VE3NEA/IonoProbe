Using Alerts

Two types of storm alerts are available in IonoProbe, WWV Alerts and Real-Time Alerts.


WWV Alerts

IonoProbe downloads the WWV Geophysical Alerts and displays them in an easy to understand way. These alerts are updated every 3 hours and cover the last 24-hour period as well as provide information about the storms expected in the next 24 hours.

Three types of storms are reported by WWV:

G0 - G5: geomagnetic storms;
S0 - S5: solar radiation storms;
R0 - R5: radio blackouts.

Storm levels are displayed with six 'lamps'. The colors of lamps change from green through yellow to red to reflect the storm level; past values are dimmed. Mouse-over hints provide text representation of the conditions.

Click on the lamps for a description of storm types and storm level codes.



Real-Time Alerts

Some space weather events, such as X-Ray bursts that cause radio blackouts, last from tens of minutes to a few hours. WWV alerts are not frequent enough to report such events in real time.

IonoProbe has its own system of alerts based on the real-time data. It monitors the X-ray Flux, Proton Flux and Kp Index and fires an alarm when the value crosses the red line. You can enable or disable the audio alarm in the Settings Dialog.

When a storm is in progress, the Systray Icon turns from blue to red , and its mouse-over hint displays the storm message.
